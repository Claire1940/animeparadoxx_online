What separates the **anime paradox god slayer** from other high-DPS units like Dangai Ichuga or Super Vegito is his intricate passive system. He is not just a "stat stick"; he requires careful placement and team composition to maximize his output. His kit is divided into three distinct passives that govern how he interacts with bosses and allies.

The most critical passive for high-difficulty content is **Red Tyrant**. This ability applies a hemorrhage effect on every attack. Hemorrhage is a multi-layered debuff that increases the damage the enemy takes by 15%, reduces their HP regeneration by 50%, and deals 5% of their maximum HP as bleed damage over 5 seconds. In game modes where bosses have millions of health points, this 5% max HP bleed is often more valuable than the unit's raw attack damage.

<Section icon={Shield} title="God Slayer vs. Death Reaper (Hades)">

In the current 2026 Eternal tier, the **anime paradox god slayer** is frequently compared to Hades (Death Reaper). While both are Eternal units with similar range and base stats, they serve fundamentally different roles in a team composition. Soji is your primary debuffer and boss killer, whereas Hades functions as a tanky bruiser with self-healing capabilities.

Hades features the "Vital Shackles" passive, which allows him to gain 35% damage permanently after taking his first hit, but at the cost of 50% of his current HP. He also possesses "Divine Physiology," providing 25% damage reduction while his health is high. Comparing the two, Soji offers more utility for the entire team through his 15% damage amplification, while Hades is a more self-sufficient unit that can survive on the front lines longer.
